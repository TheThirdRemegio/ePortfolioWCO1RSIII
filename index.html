Case Studies

S. Back at Cebu Genomics, our clinical team was manually reading through massive papers of genomic data from legacy sequencing machines. This process was a large bottleneck on efficiency, both being highly prone to human error and delaying report generation by days at a time.

T. Develop a new QC pipeline based on a new, up-to-date Python library to parse raw genomic data according to specific control flags. Then: send the data downstream towards the clinical team for analysis.

A. I developed a set of custom Python scripts using the Pandas library (known for data manipulation and analysis) to handle the relatively high volumes of data to parse. I wrote in logic, documentation rationalizing each step of said logic (for future technicians), and integrated the new QC pipeline from the bottom-up. Validated data would now be flagged then packaged in multiple JSON and text formats.

R. By shifting from manual entry to an automated script, data throughput was at an all-time high in the genomics facility, though QC slowly became the new bottleneck as sensitive sections of data still needed to be checked by live technicians and analysts. Overall, manual processing time appeared to be cut down by over 50%.

S. Cebu Genomic's legacy LIS was suffering from a lack of updates and a development cycle that had ended long ago. With each new patient entry, the proportion of "orphaned" records — patient files that reference non-existing data, and vice-versa — was ever-increasing.

T. Audit and automate data extraction while keeping the legacy machines stable. Also: ensure the updated system(s) and data back-ups complied with BRAP standards, as per the initial expectations for my hiring.

A. In line with this, I conducted a deep-clean of the database by installing auditing systems along a new, improved QC pipeline. I also revised the role-access matrix in the laboratory and established more secure transfer protocols, for the careful sharing sensitive of patient biodata with our top five client-hospitals.

R. This initiative improved the overall integrity of the LIS and prepared it for possibly the next five years of the LIS' development cycle. A more robust, secure, and user-friendly Electronic Health Records (EHR) system was thereafter integrated into the facility using the updated LIS backbone, securing compliance with BRAP principles.
